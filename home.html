WELCOME TO UDUPI

Udupi district, which stands as a symbol for temple and cultural tradition, is a small district comprising three taluks. On the west it is bounded by the roaring sea and to its east reclines the dense green Sahyadri mountain ranges. The district is blessed with a fairly 100 km long beautiful coastline. Malpe forms the only big natural port which, of late, is developing into a large fishing port. Dotted with tall coconut trees, the St. Mary's Island and the neighbouring island Bahadurgadh are the tourist attractions located about 10 km from Malpe beach. Kapu which is 11 km from Udupi is yet another attraction with gorgeous beach and a light house built during the British period. Maravanthe, is another tourist spot, where the national highway passes quietly. The river Sauparnika flows glutted with water towards the right, while, the sea roaring on the left looks as though kissing the beach. The turtle rearing centre on the beach of Maravanthe attracts the tourists, nature lovers and those who are interested in exploring biodiversity.

HOW TO REACH UDUPI

BY ROAD

Udupi is the city which is well connected to neighboring cities (Mangalore, Shimoga, etc) of Karnataka.

BY TRAIN

Udupi is well connected by Railways. Konkan Railway route passes through the district(Udupi) connecting major cities of India.

BY AIR

There is no airport in Udupi district.The nearest airport is Mangalore International Airport located about 59.4 kms away from Udupi.
