MOOKAMBIKA WILDLIFE SANTUARY

Mookambika Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected wildlife sanctuary in the southern state of Karnataka in India. It derives its name from the presiding deity Goddess Mookambika of the popular Kollur Mookambika Temple. The sanctuary lies in the Western Ghats in Udupi district of Karnataka.

About
  • The sanctuary consists of an area of 370.37 km sq
  • Established in the year 1974
  • Outlook Traveler has rated Maravanthe as Karnataka’s most beautiful beach in 2005.
Facilities

  • Places to Stay:Options are available to stay in Kollur
  • Area:< 370.4 sq km
  • Nearest city: Kundapur

SOMESHWARA WILDLIFE SANTUARY

Someshwara Wildlife Sanctuary is a protected wildlife sanctuary in the Western Ghats of Karnataka state in India. It is named after the presiding deity "Lord Someshwara" of the famed Someshwara temple located within the sanctuary.

About
  • Established in 1974
  • The Nearest town is Hebri which is connected by bus service to Udupi
  • The sanctuary houses Sitanadi nature camp run by Karnataka Forest Department
Info
  • Timimg: Mon-Sun: 06:00AM-06:00PM
  • Nearest Town: Udupi
  • Best Season to visit: November to April
  • Video Camera Fee: Free
  • Still Camera Fee: Free
  • Entry Fee: Adult: Rs. 200 Child: Rs. 100/-

MALYADI BIRD SANTUARY

Malyadi Bird Sanctuary is an abandoned clay query 9 kms away from Kundapura. Malyadi is one of the few places in Udupi District where one can see the large congregations of birds,particularly during summer months

About
  • Malyadi is 24kms away from Udupi
  • This marshland was created as a result of excessive rain water accumulation over excavated land
  • Some of the birds seen here are Whistling Teals, Purple Moorhen, White Ibis, Comb duck, Carmorants and Darters
Info
  • Nearest Town: Kundapur
  • Entry Fee: Free
  • Area: 1.5 sq kms
